Court System Structure

The US Judicial Branch is divided into two systems: federal and state.

Federal Court System:

1. U.S. District Courts: Handles cases involving federal law.
2. U.S. Courts of Appeals: Reviews decisions from district courts in their disricts
3. U.S. Supreme Court: The highest court, holding authority over every other court.


State Court Systems:

States have their own, unique courts due to the varying state laws.

1. Trial Courts: Handle civil and criminal cases.
2. Intermediate Appellate Courts: Review decisions from trial courts.
3. State Supreme Courts: The highest state court, only below the federal supreme court.
