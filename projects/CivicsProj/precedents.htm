Previous Cases & Precedents

Many cases have changed how the law works in the US, here are two notable ones:

1. Brown v. Board of Education (1954): This case caused the court to determine racial
segregation in public schools unconstitutional, overturning the "separate but equal" ruling from
Plessy v. Ferguson (1896).

2. Roe v. Wade (1973): A woman was unable to have an abortion in Texas, and took it to
court. The Court ruled that it was a woman's constitutional right to privacy, legalizing abortion
nationwide. However, in Dobbs v. Jackson (2022), the Supreme Court overturned Roe v. Wade,
removing the federal right to abortion for women and leaving the legality to a state-by-state basis.
